Anemia Detection using Multilayer Perceptron

A web-based application designed to assist healthcare professionals in the early detection and classification of anemia. The system utilizes a Multilayer Perceptron (MLP) machine learning model to analyze Complete Blood Count (CBC) results and classify anemia into three types: microcytic, macrocytic, and normocytic. The goal of the project is to provide a faster, more accurate, and accessible decision-support tool that enhances diagnostic efficiency while complementing, not replacing, traditional medical assessments.

Casa Kape Online Ordering System

This project proposes the development of a custom web-based online ordering platform for Casa Kape that allows customers to browse the menu and place orders directly without relying on third-party applications. The system includes an admin dashboard where the owner can manage orders, track sales in real time, and monitor monthly profits. The platform aims to streamline operations, reduce manual errors from logbooks, improve customer experience, and provide the business with better control and data-driven insights for growth.

WHAT SHOULD I DO RIGHT

NOW?

“What Should I Do Right Now?” is a minimal and calming web application designed to offer gentle, realistic suggestions for the present moment. Instead of focusing on productivity or tracking progress, the platform encourages small, manageable actions that help users reset, breathe, and move forward without pressure. Through simple mood-based filtering and soft design elements, the website creates a supportive digital space that promotes balance, self-compassion, and mindful decision-making in everyday situations.
